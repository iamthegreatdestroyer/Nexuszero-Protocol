## General Principles

### What Trademarks Protect

Trademarks protect:
- Brand identity and reputation
- Consumer recognition
- Association with quality
- Prevention of confusion

Trademarks do NOT protect:
- The underlying technology (patents protect that)
- The source code (copyright protects that)
- Ideas or concepts

### Proper Use Protects Everyone

Proper trademark use:
- Maintains brand value
- Prevents dilution
- Avoids consumer confusion
- Protects against generic use

## Allowed Uses (No Permission Required)

You may use our trademarks WITHOUT permission for:

### 1. Factual References

**Example - Allowed**:
- "Built with NexusZero Protocol"
- "Compatible with NexusZero"
- "Integrates with NexusZero Protocol"
- "Uses NexusZero cryptography"

**Guidelines**:
- Use the full trademark ("NexusZero Protocol", not just "NexusZero")
- Use as an adjective, not a noun ("NexusZero Protocol software", not "a NexusZero")
- Don't imply endorsement ("Powered by NexusZero" OK, "Approved by NexusZero" NOT OK)

### 2. Educational Content

**Example - Allowed**:
- "Tutorial: Getting Started with NexusZero Protocol"
- "NexusZero Protocol: A Technical Overview"
- "Comparing NexusZero Protocol to Other ZK Systems"
- "NexusZero Protocol Developer Guide"

**Guidelines**:
- Must be genuinely educational, not promotional
- Clearly indicate if unofficial/community content
- Don't suggest official affiliation
- Provide accurate information

### 3. Open Source Projects

**Example - Allowed**:
- "nexuszero-cli: Community CLI tool for NexusZero Protocol"
- "NexusZero Protocol Rust Bindings"
- "Community Dashboard for NexusZero Protocol"

**Guidelines**:
- Clearly identify as community/third-party project
- Don't use "NexusZero" or "NexusZero Protocol" as the main name
- Format: "[Your Project Name]: [Description] for NexusZero Protocol"
- Include disclaimer: "Not officially affiliated with NexusZero Protocol"

### 4. News and Commentary

**Example - Allowed**:
- "NexusZero Protocol Launches Mainnet"
- "Review: NexusZero Protocol's Quantum-Resistant ZK System"
- "Analysis of NexusZero Protocol's Performance"

**Guidelines**:
- Use in factual reporting
- Nominative fair use applies
- Don't imply endorsement
- Clearly distinguish editorial from advertising

### 5. Academic and Research

**Example - Allowed**:
- "Cryptanalysis of NexusZero Protocol's Lattice Implementation"
- "Performance Benchmarking: NexusZero Protocol vs Competitors"
- "Security Analysis of NexusZero Protocol"

**Guidelines**:
- Scholarly use is fair use
- Accurate representation of technology
- Proper citation
- Non-commercial academic use

## Uses Requiring Permission

You MUST request permission for:

### 1. Product Names

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- "NexusZero Enterprise Edition"
- "NexusZero Cloud"
- "NexusZero Plus"

**Why**: Could create confusion about official products

**Alternative**: "[Your Company] Enterprise Platform for NexusZero Protocol"

### 2. Domain Names

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- nexuszero.com
- nexuszero.io
- nexuszero-protocol.com
- nexuszero-anything.com

**Why**: Could be confused with official sites

**Alternative**: [yourcompany]-nexuszero.com or nexuszero-[description].com (with permission)

### 3. Company Names

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- "NexusZero Corporation"
- "NexusZero Technologies, Inc."
- "NexusZero Labs"

**Why**: Implies official affiliation or ownership

**Alternative**: "[Your Name] NexusZero Consulting" or "NexusZero Solutions by [Your Company]" (with permission)

### 4. Logo Usage

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- Using NexusZero logo on your product
- Using NexusZero logo on your website
- Using NexusZero logo in marketing materials
- Modified versions of NexusZero logo

**Why**: Logos are highly distinctive and protected

**Alternative**: Text references without logo, or request official partnership

### 5. Commercial Products

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- Selling products/services with "NexusZero" in the name
- Merchandising (t-shirts, mugs, etc.)
- Training courses/certifications with "NexusZero" branding

**Why**: Commercial use of trademarks requires licensing

**How**: Contact enterprise@nexuszero.io for licensing

### 6. Modified Versions

**Example - Requires Permission**:
- "NexusZero Community Edition"
- "NexusZero Fork" (as a product name)
- "Enhanced NexusZero"

**Why**: Could confuse users about official vs. modified versions

**Alternative**: "[Your Name]'s NexusZero Protocol Fork" or "Community Fork of NexusZero Protocol by [Your Name]"

## Prohibited Uses

You may NOT:

### 1. Confusing or Misleading Use

**Prohibited**:
- Implying you are NexusZero Protocol or an official representative
- Suggesting official endorsement when there is none
- Using trademarks in a way that confuses users
- Claiming to be "official" without authorization

### 2. Disparaging Use

**Prohibited**:
- Using trademarks to criticize or disparage NexusZero Protocol
- Associating trademarks with illegal, obscene, or offensive content
- Using in a way that damages our reputation

**Note**: You CAN write critical reviews/commentary using fair use, but don't use trademarks in the title of disparaging content.

### 3. Modified Trademarks

**Prohibited**:
- Altering the spelling ("NexusZer0", "Nexus Zero")
- Combining with other words to create new marks ("NexusZeroify", "NexusZeronic")
- Translating to other languages without permission
- Creating similar-sounding marks

### 4. Generic Use

**Prohibited**:
- Using "NexusZero" as a generic term for ZK protocols
- Using as a verb ("We NexusZeroed the transaction")
- Using as a common noun ("a nexuszero", "nexuszeros")

**Why**: Generic use can cause loss of trademark protection

### 5. Domain Squatting

**Prohibited**:
- Registering domains with "NexusZero" for resale
- Registering typo domains (nexuzero.com, nexuxzero.com)
- Registering for bad faith purposes

**Consequences**: UDRP proceedings, potential litigation

### Attribution and Notice

**Include this notice** in prominent location (footer, about page, README):

```

```

**For products/services**:
```
[Your Product] is built with NexusZero Protocol technology.
NexusZero and NexusZero Protocol are trademarks of NexusZero Protocol.
This product is not officially endorsed by NexusZero Protocol.
```

### Proper Format

**Correct**:
- "NexusZero Protocol software"
- "the NexusZero Protocol system"
- "NexusZero Protocol cryptography"

**Incorrect**:
- "a NexusZero Protocol" (don't use as noun)
- "NexusZeroing" (don't use as verb)
- "NexusZero Protocols" (don't pluralize)

### Visual Presentation

**Text**:
- Use proper capitalization: "NexusZero", not "nexuszero" or "NEXUSZERO"
- Don't abbreviate: "NexusZero", not "NZ" or "NZP"
- Use distinct formatting (bold, italics) for first use

**Logo**:
- Use official logo artwork only (request from marketing@nexuszero.io)
- Don't resize disproportionately
- Maintain clear space around logo
- Don't change colors
- Don't add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)

## Requesting Permission

### How to Request

**Email**: trademarks@nexuszero.io

**Include**:
1. **Your Information**: Name, company, contact details
2. **Intended Use**: Detailed description of how you'll use the trademark
3. **Context**: Product/service description, target audience
4. **Duration**: How long you need permission
5. **Materials**: Mockups, samples, or examples of proposed use
6. **Commercial/Non-commercial**: Will this be used for profit?

**Response Time**: Typically 5-7 business days

### What to Expect

**Approval**:
- Written permission (email sufficient for most uses)
- Conditions of use
- Duration of permission
- Attribution requirements

**Conditional Approval**:
- Modifications required
- Additional guidelines
- Re-submit revised materials

**Denial**:
- Reason for denial
- Alternative suggestions
- Option to resubmit with changes

### Partnership Programs

**Official Partners**:
- Enhanced trademark usage rights
- Co-marketing opportunities
- Logo usage permission
- "Official Partner" designation

**Contact**: partnerships@nexuszero.io

## FAQ

### Q: Can I use "NexusZero" in my GitHub repository name?

**A**: Yes, if it's clearly a tool/library for NexusZero Protocol and not presented as an official project. Format: "[your-tool]-nexuszero" or "nexuszero-[your-tool]". Include disclaimer in README.

### Q: Can I make NexusZero t-shirts?

**A**: Not without permission. Merchandising requires licensing. Contact trademarks@nexuszero.io.

### Q: Can I use the NexusZero logo in a presentation?

**A**: Yes, for educational presentations. Request official logo from marketing@nexuszero.io. Must include attribution.

### Q: What if I already registered a domain with "NexusZero" in it?

**A**: Contact trademarks@nexuszero.io immediately. We may allow it with conditions, or ask you to transfer/redirect.

### Q: Can I call my product "NexusZero Compatible"?

**A**: Yes, if it's truly compatible. Format: "[Your Product] - NexusZero Protocol Compatible"

### Q: What about derivative terms like "NexusZeronic" or "NexusZeroify"?

**A**: Not allowed. These create confusing similar marks.

### Q: Can I translate the name for my local market?

**A**: Not without permission. Contact trademarks@nexuszero.io to discuss.

### Q: What if I disagree with these guidelines?

**A**: Contact trademarks@nexuszero.io to discuss. We're reasonable and want to enable community use while protecting our brand.
